Harmonograph: Lissajious patterns in real life


We can produce lissajious patterns using a pendulum by varying its frequency along x and y (for example North-South and East-West) directions. As the frequency of pendulum is dependent on its length, we can have an experimental setup through which we can vary frequency along different axes.
Watch this video for full details of experiment

Since in real world the amplitude of the pendulum keeps decreasing with time before the pendulum comes to stop, we see variations of Lissajious patterns. Play with the patterns by varying damping values and number of iterations(each iteration is one to and fro swing of the pendulum).
