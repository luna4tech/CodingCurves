Learn about Lissajious curves


A circle is formed by plotting sin and cos of values on the xy coordinates. Since the values of sin and cos range between -1 and 1, we get a circle of radius of 1. Multiplying the values by a fixed value r gives a circle of radius r.
x = r cos θ
y = r sin θ

An ellipse is formed by plotting the points like in circle but with different radius across x and y axes.
x = A cos θ
y = B sin θ

Above curves have a frequency of 1. By varying the frequency of the cos and sin waves in x and y directions, we see interesting patterns which are called Lissajious curves. We already know that cos and sin are phase different by 90 degrees. We can vary phase difference between the horizontal and vertical waves to see even more different patterns. Below are the equations to represent these patterns.
x = A sin (ω1 θ) + φ
y = B sin (ω2 θ)
